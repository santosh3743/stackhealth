# Code of Conduct

StackHealth is an open project, and we want it to be a useful place to
work for everyone who shows up to help — regardless of who they are or
where they're starting from.

## Our standards

We expect everyone who participates in this project (issues, pull
requests, discussions, code reviews, or any other forum tied to the
project) to follow a small number of straightforward principles.

**Be respectful.** Disagreements about technical decisions are welcome
and expected. Disagreements about people are not. Critique the idea,
not the person.

**Be specific.** "This is wrong" is not feedback. "This breaks on Windows
because of path-separator handling at line 42" is. Specificity is how we
move forward.

**Assume good intent.** People come to open source for different reasons
and with different backgrounds. Read messages charitably. Ask for
clarification before reacting.

**No personal attacks or harassment.** This includes insults, threats,
sustained disruption, derogatory remarks based on personal
characteristics, or attempts to dox or shame anyone. This is a clear
red line.

**Stay on topic.** Issue threads, pull requests, and discussions are
about the project. Avoid bringing in unrelated personal, political, or
ideological arguments — there are plenty of other places online for
those.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies anywhere the project is being discussed or
worked on: this repository, any official chat channels, project-related
events, and any space where someone is acting on behalf of the project.

## Reporting

If you see something that crosses one of the lines above, please email
the maintainer privately:

> **santosh3743@gmail.com**

Include:
- A description of what happened
- Links to the relevant comments/messages if any
- Anything else you think is relevant

Reports will be kept confidential. We will respond within 5 business
days. The maintainer will take whatever action seems appropriate — from
a private conversation to a temporary or permanent ban from project
spaces.

If the report involves the maintainer, please send it to someone else
you trust in the open-source community to escalate, or open a GitHub
abuse report.

## Enforcement

A first incident usually gets a private warning. A pattern of behavior
gets a ban. The maintainer reserves the right to skip the warning step
if the conduct is egregious enough.

We do not promise a formal appeals process. If you think a decision was
unfair, you can explain your view by email; we will read it and decide.
